Blindfold Build Activity

Building Challenge (10-15 minutes)

  • The Guide looks at the example structure and describes the steps to recreate it.
  • The Builders, who are blindfolded, must rely entirely on the Guide’s instructions without being able to see the structure or the pieces.
  • Builders should communicate back to the Guide if they’re unsure or need clarification, practicing trust in their teammate’s directions.

What Students Learn from the Blindfold Build Activity

  1. Trust and Reliance on Others: Builders must rely entirely on the Guide’s instructions, building trust in their teammate's ability to provide clear and accurate guidance. This reliance fosters a sense of interdependence that is essential for effective teamwork.
  2. Clear and Concise Communication: The Guide learns to communicate instructions clearly and simply, avoiding assumptions and ensuring every direction is understandable. Builders, in turn, practice communicating back to verify instructions, improving two-way communication.
  3. Active Listening: Builders must listen attentively to the Guide’s instructions without visual cues. This challenge emphasizes the importance of active listening skills in teamwork, as misunderstandings can easily arise without them.
  4. Patience and Adaptability: Both roles require patience—Builders need to work at a pace that allows them to process instructions without visual aid, while the Guide has to adapt instructions based on the Builders’ responses and needs.
  5. Problem-Solving and Collaboration Under Constraints: Working with limited resources (blindfolds and verbal-only instructions) encourages the team to find creative solutions and collaborate closely, fostering a stronger team dynamic.
  6. Empathy and Perspective-Taking: Experiencing the challenges of both roles helps students empathize with their teammates, recognizing the difficulties in guiding others and following without seeing.

Together, these skills reinforce a stronger foundation for teamwork, improving each student’s ability to communicate, trust, and collaborate effectively in various settings.
